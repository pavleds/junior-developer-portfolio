The problem

Enthusiast car listings are exciting, but comparing options across price, power, transmission, and body style can quickly become noisy. Buyers need an easy way to narrow a collection without losing the emotion of the cars.

Apex Garage is a fictional front-end marketplace concept. Its inventory, prices, finance figures, and enquiries are demonstrations only.

The approach

I combined a cinematic visual direction with practical shopping tools. The interface keeps the collection aspirational while making every next step obvious.

Fast discovery

Search, brand, transmission, and price controls update the inventory immediately.

Shortlisting

Saved cars persist between visits through browser storage.

Clear comparison

Buyers can place two cars side by side and compare the specifications that matter.

Useful next steps

An illustrative finance calculator and enquiry form complete the browsing journey.

Technical details

Data-driven inventory

Every listing is generated from a JavaScript vehicle object, keeping filtering, details, comparison, finance, and saved state connected to one source of truth.

Persistent favourites

Saved vehicle IDs are serialized into localStorage. The header count and listing buttons update from the same state after every action.

Accessible interactions

Native dialog elements provide focused details and enquiry flows, while labelled form controls, keyboard-friendly buttons, and reduced-motion support make the experience easier to operate.

Production boundaries

A real marketplace would require a secure backend, authenticated dealer accounts, database inventory, image storage, server-side validation, compliant finance integrations, and protected customer data.

What I learned

  • A strong visual identity works best when it supports rather than competes with the buying task.
  • One structured dataset can power several interface features without duplicating logic.
  • Comparison limits should be visible and forgiving, with clear feedback when reached.
  • Demo finance tools must explain their assumptions and avoid presenting estimates as offers.

The outcome

A polished, responsive automotive marketplace prototype with live filters, persistent favourites, two-car comparison, vehicle details, an illustrative finance estimate, and an enquiry flow.
